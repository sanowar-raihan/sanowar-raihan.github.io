Research and Publications
Random Meshes
SGI Research Blog

Given the boundary of a shape, we can generate random triangles to tessellate the interior. Even with gaps and intersections, algorithms like the heat method are well-defined on such meshes.

How to Mend a Broken Part
SGI Research Blog

We study the problem of geometric fracture assembly and design an evaluation metric to compare different assembly algorithms. We also host an open-source challenge for the same task.
The (in)accurate Gradients of Neural Representations
SGI Research Blog

While solving time-dependent PDEs, neural network-based implicit representations suffer from noisy gradients. We explore several strategies like finite differences and time averaging to solve this problem.
Underwater Dense Scene Reconstruction and Mapping
SGI Research Blog

We develop an SFM pipeline for 3D reconstruction of the underwater seafloor. We used the ROV data collected by the Seattle Aquarium to create a map of the underwater Kelp forest.
S2C-DeLeNet: A parameter transfer based segmentation-classification integration for detecting skin cancer lesions from dermoscopic images

Md. Jahin Alam, Mir Sayeed Mohammad, Md Adnan Faisal Hossain, Ishtiaque Ahmed Showmik, Munshi Sanowar Raihan, Shahed Ahmed, Talha Ibn Mahmud
